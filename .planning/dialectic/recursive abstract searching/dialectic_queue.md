# Dialectic Queue: Recursive Abstract Searching

## Explored Contradictions

### Round 1: Does Verifiability Redeem Autonomy?
- **Monk A:** Verifiable autonomy is a proof-generating instrument (telescope, not oracle)
- **Monk B:** Verifiability is a seductive alibi; the judgment itself is the crossing
- **Synthesis:** The Persistence Hunter Architecture — agent as endurance, human as tracker at forks
- **Status:** SUPERSEDED by Round 2 — the architecture was identified as managed decline ("a little death")
- **Files:** `monk_a_verifiable_autonomy.md`, `monk_b_guardian_of_judgment.md`, `sublation_round1.md`

### Round 2: What Does Serapeum Owe the Researcher When the Tracker Is No Longer Needed?
- **Monk A:** The architecture is a transitional object (chrysalis, not hospice); researchers will evolve
- **Monk B:** The architecture is a hospice; if you can't preserve generative capacity, don't build
- **Synthesis:** THE ARRAKIS PRINCIPLE — the agent must make research harder, not easier; it is a problem generator, not an answer generator
- **Status:** VALIDATED — both monks elevated, hostile auditor found 70% genuine insight
- **Files:** `monk_a_r2_transitional_architect.md`, `monk_b_r2_salt_guardian.md`, `sublation_round2.md`, `synthesis_final.md`

## Open Contradictions (Queued)

### The Bootstrapping Problem (Auditor's Harder Contradiction)
Who trains the researcher to evaluate machine-generated problems when the training pipeline (manual traversal) is disrupted? What does research pedagogy look like on Arrakis? Is this Serapeum's problem or the institution's?
- **Source:** Convergent critique from all three Round 2 validators
- **Priority:** High — but may belong to a different stakeholder
- **Status:** DEFERRED — identified as institutional, not tool-design

### Spice Dependency
The new Arrakis skills only work at agent-provided scale. If the agent fails or becomes unavailable, the researcher has skills optimized for a tool that no longer exists. Is this a new form of the dependency the Farsi proverb warned about?
- **Source:** Round 2 new contradictions
- **Status:** QUEUED — partially addressed by "Arrakis skills work on Caladan at smaller scale" but not fully resolved

### Who Controls the Desert?
Whoever builds the agent controls what problems researchers see. The political economy shifts from "who funds the researcher?" to "who builds the worm?" Serapeum's answer (local-first, open-source) addresses this for one tool but not for the ecosystem.
- **Source:** Round 2 new contradictions
- **Status:** QUEUED — partially addressed by Serapeum's local-first architecture

### The Slopacolypse Feedback Loop
Autonomous agents → more low-quality papers → larger corpus → more need for agents → more low-quality papers. The tool and the problem co-amplify. Does the Arrakis Principle accelerate or resist this loop?
- **Source:** Round 1 new contradiction, Karpathy's "slopacolypse" prediction
- **Status:** QUEUED — not yet engaged

### The Culling Question
If the Caladan-to-Arrakis transition is real, how many researchers survive it? Is the outcome transformation of a profession or bifurcation into a small Fremen elite and a large displaced population?
- **Source:** Monk B Round 2 validation
- **Status:** QUEUED — existential but may be beyond Serapeum's design scope
